Stay Calm & Compliant PhysicallyKeep your hands visible. Do not resist, even if you believe you're being treated unlawfully.

Invoke Your Right to Remain SilentClearly state: "I am invoking my right to remain silent." Then stop talking.
✓
Ask "Am I Free to Go?"If you're not under arrest, you have the right to leave. Ask clearly and calmly.

What You Must Never Do
NEVER
DO THIS.
✕
Never Consent to a SearchPolitely say "I do not consent to this search." A warrant is required. Consenting waives your rights.
✕
Never Talk to Police Without a LawyerEven if you are innocent. Anything you say can — and will — be used against you in court.
✕
Never Lie to Law EnforcementLying to police is a crime. Your only safe options are silence or honesty through counsel.
✕
Never Physically Resist an ArrestEven an unlawful arrest. Fight it in court — not on the street. Resistance escalates and charges you further.
✕
Never Post About It on Social MediaAnything you post is public record and can be subpoenaed. Stay completely silent online until your case is resolved.

01
Traffic Stop
THE ROUTINE PULL-OVER

"You were going 7 over. License and registration. Mind if I take a look in your car while I run these?"

What most people do
Say "sure, I have nothing to hide" — opening the door to a full search and anything found being used against them.
Jake's Rule
"I do not consent to any search of my vehicle." — Politely. Firmly. Every time.
02
Home Encounter
THE 3AM KNOCK

"Police. Open up. We just need to ask you a few questions about your neighbor."

What most people do
Open the door wide, invite them in, and begin talking — allowing officers to observe everything inside.
Jake's Rule
Step outside, close the door. "Do you have a warrant?" Without one, you do not have to let them in.
03
Street Detention
YOU MATCH A DESCRIPTION

"Stop right there. You match the description of someone we're looking for. Where are you coming from?"

What most people do
Explain themselves at length, answer every question, and consent to a pat-down to "show they're cooperating."
Jake's Rule
"Am I being detained or am I free to go?" Stay silent beyond that. Hands visible. Do not run.
04
Workplace Arrest
DETECTIVES AT YOUR JOB

"We're investigating a matter and would just like a few minutes of your time. It's nothing serious."

What most people do
Agree to talk privately, answer questions freely, and try to explain away suspicion — often creating new ones.
Jake's Rule
"I'm happy to cooperate with my attorney present." Then call immediately. Do not speak first.
05
DUI Checkpoint
THE CHECKPOINT STOP

"Evening. How much have you had to drink tonight? Where are you coming from?"

What most people do
Answer honestly — "just two beers" — which is often used as probable cause to further detain and test.
Jake's Rule
Provide license and registration. "I am invoking my right to remain silent." You are not required to answer.
